# Estimating Minecraft Image-Art Materials Before Building

Image art can become much larger than expected after scale and palette choices are applied. A material estimate should happen before the final block collection trip.

Choose the finished width and height, then decide whether one source pixel maps to one block or a larger square. Remove transparent background areas from the count and separate structural support blocks from visible palette blocks.

Review the palette for materials that are difficult to obtain, directional, affected by gravity, or visually different under lighting. Replace rare shades only after checking how the simplified image looks as a whole. A locally accurate color is not always worth a major collection detour.

Prepare the source with [Minecraft Image Art](https://mcimgart.com), export or record the palette counts, and add a small reserve for placement mistakes. Organize materials by build section so the inventory order follows the construction order.
