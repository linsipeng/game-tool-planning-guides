# Minecraft Enchantment Order Misconceptions

## “Identical books always have identical anvil cost”

Books carrying different prior-work histories can produce different costs despite matching enchantments.

## “The order of the two anvil slots does not matter”

Reversing base and sacrifice can change the operation cost or legality.

## “Combining everything into one book is always cheapest”

An unbalanced pairing tree can accumulate a large prior-work penalty before the final item.

## “Only the final operation cost matters”

The total sequence cost and earlier legal limits must also be considered.

## “A calculator can infer previous operations from enchantments”

Prior work is historical data and must be entered explicitly.
