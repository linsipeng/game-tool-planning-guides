# Auditing Minecraft Enchanted Books Before Using an Anvil

Anvil planning becomes confusing when books have similar names but different prior-work histories. A short inventory audit prevents accidental expensive combinations.

Give every book a unique label and record its enchantments, level, prior anvil uses, and whether it can still be duplicated or replaced. Keep naturally obtained books separate from books already combined several times. Two visually identical books may have very different future costs.

Next, mark incompatible enchantments and the final item restrictions. Build the combination tree from cheap, low-history inputs and compare alternative pairings before performing any operation. Do not rely on the order books happen to occupy in a chest.

Use the [Minecraft Enchantment Order Calculator](https://enchantorder.com) to test the labeled inputs and proposed sequence. After each real anvil operation, update the inventory record. This keeps the remaining plan aligned with the actual prior-work state.
